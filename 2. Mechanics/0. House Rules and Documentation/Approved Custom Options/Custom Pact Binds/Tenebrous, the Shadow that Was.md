# Tenebrous, the Shadow that Was

## Legend
In aeons past, a great demon lord had long sought divinity. For a brief period, he actually attained it. Slain and resurrected by a surge of negative energy, the corpulent demon arose as the gaunt Tenebrous, a god of darkness and undeath. For a time, he traveled the planes in this form, slaying other divine beings in his quest to gain more power. Some say he was thwarted by a band of mortal heroes, but whatever the cause, his grand plan failed. 

True divinity can never fade completely. The tatters of godly power that he had shed remained intact. Less than a god but still divine, this bit of essence drifted in the void between planes until it once more coalesced into a bitter sentience. Thus, Tenebrous yet exists as a pale reflection of what he once was, a shadow of a shadow.

## Summoning Rules
The following describes the requirements and rituals for sealing pacts with Tenebrous
- Spirit Level: 4th
- Alignment: CE
- Constellation: Fiend
- Binding DC: 25
### Totems
You gain a +2 totem bonus on binding checks made with Tenebrous one of the following requirements is met, or a +4 bonus if all requirements are met.

- The binder possesses at least 5 ranks in both Spellcraft and Knowledge Arcana.   
- The binder places a red gemstone worth at least 100 gp (ruby, garnet, red tourmaline, etc.) and places it in the center of the seal. 
- The binder places an arcane scroll of at least 3rd level in the seal that is consumed as part of the binding ceremony. 
### Ceremony
The binder must draw Tenebrous's seal at night or in deep shadow, using ash mixed with their own blood — a fitting medium for a being caught between death and existence. The seal should be drawn in silence; no words are spoken until the rite formally begins.
### Manifestation
Upon completion of the rite to summon Tenebrous, the summoner's shadow shifts to fall across the seal. Even if the rite occurs in complete darkness, the shadow is visible as a darker spot in the blackness. Once the shadow crosses into the seal, an inky humanoid form - impossibly gaunt, holding its limbs at disjointed angles - rises from it. The voice of Tenebrous is a whisper in the wind, almost impossible to hear, yet laden with unmistakable meaning.

## Major Granted Abilities

### Turn/Command Undead
You can Turn or Command undead as a cleric of your effective binder level. This ability has an effective range of 30’ radius.  As with a cleric, you turn if your alignment is good and command if you are evil. If you are neutral, you choose whether to turn or command upon binding with Tenebrous and for the duration of the pact. If you have the ability to turn or command undead from other classes, your levels in those classes stack with your effective binder level for the purpose of determining your turning ability. A Will Save negates this effect. 

- Turn Undead - Undead that fail their save flee for 1 minute. Intelligent undead receive a new saving throw each round to end the effect.
- Command Undead -  Undead that fail their saves fall under your control, obeying your commands to the best of their ability, as if under the effects of [control undead](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/magic/all-spells/c/control-undead). Intelligent undead receive a new saving throw each day to resist your command. You can control any number of undead, so long as their total Hit Dice do not exceed your binder level
Once you have used this ability, you cannot do so again for 5 rounds.

**Capstone Empowerment:** The effective range of Turn/Command Undead is extended to 60’ radius. 

## Minor Granted Abilities

### Deeper Darkness
You can cloak an area in shadows as though using the deeper darkness spell (caster level equals your effective binder level), with the following exceptions: The effect is always centered on you, and you can shift the range of the emanation (within the normal range) up or down 10 feet as a move action. You can suppress or activate this ability as a standard action.

### See in Darkness
You can see perfectly through darkness of any kind, even that created by a deeper darkness spell (or your deeper darkness ability).

### Touch of the Void
As a swift action, you can charge a single melee or ranged weapon attack with profane damage. Your next melee attack deals an extra 1d8 points of profane energy damage, plus 1d8 points of profane energy damage for every four effective binder levels beyond 7th that you possess. When you attain an effective binder level of 11th, you can charge your weapon for an entire round.  The binder may use this ability 3+ Cha modifier times a day.  (Profane Damage as described here - Profane damage comes from attacks that are heavily infused with the power of sheer evil and carry this unholy power over as a form of harmful energy to good-aligned beings. Profane damage deals full damage to incorporeal targets and deals damage as if the weapon were evil-aligned. As it is not a weapon damage type, it automatically bypasses all damage reduction.)

### Vessel of Emptiness
You may use Shadow Step as the spell.  You must be in an area of Dim or darker illumination to use this ability.  The binder may use this ability 3+ Cha modifier times a day. 

## Signs and Influence
Tenebrous affects you in the following ways:
**Physical Sign:** You seem to be standing in shadow even on the brightest day. Furthermore, your own shadow never extends more than a few feet from your body, even if the ambient light suggests that it should be much longer. This effect does not grant you concealment.
**Personality Influence:** While influenced by Tenebrous, you are filled with a sense of detachment and an aching feeling of loss and abandonment. Tenebrous requires that you never be the first to act in combat. If your initiative check result is the highest, you must delay until someone else takes a turn.
**Favored Ally:** Outsiders (evil)
**Favored Enemy:** Outsiders (good) 
### Vestigial Bond (Companion):  
You gain a Quasit as an Improved familiar.  You possess a wizard level equal to your binder level when determining your familiar’s abilities. This granted ability replaces Vessel of Emptiness.
