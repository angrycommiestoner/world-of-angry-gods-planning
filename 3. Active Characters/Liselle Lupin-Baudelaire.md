Level 9 Ghost Sovereign Soul Weaver/Triple Goddess Hedgewitch

A member and current leader of House Lupin-Baudelaire, this youngest sister and Ustalavic Noblewoman is finding her place in this world, and coming to terms with her role in her family’s unique history.

# History
## The Friendly Ghost

Liselle was born to house Lupin-Baudelaire next in a long line of Lupin-Baudelaire children marked by the power of Desna in a strange and unique way. She was born with no body to call her own. Long did this tragic accident of birth haunt her, unable to feel the warmth of her loved ones’ embrace, Liselle grew up sharing the minds and inhabiting the bodies of others, and the time in between refining her mental acuity and control.

For twenty-one years, she sought to know and understand more, and she would occasionally catch glimpses of information when she would share her mother, the former matriarch of the Lupin-Baudelaire’s mind, but they were frustratingly shallow, and mother’s reprimands for reaching deeper into her mind than she knew should were always swift.

It was late in her twenty-first year when Liselle first manifested a familiar, a beautiful, inky, raven-like creature made of pure star-stuff, whose feathers twinkled like a thousand jewels of light were contained within them… It wasn’t the body she had long sought, but it was still more freedom than Liselle had ever had.

## An Encounter in the Shudderwood
As Liselle became more and more comfortable inhabiting the spirit of her familiar, eventually showing the creature first to her sister [[Bernadette Lupin-Baudelaire|Bernie]], then [[Elise Lupin-Baudelaire|Elise]], she found herself at last sneaking away to go on adventures *with* them. No longer bound to the confines of a locket, or the inside of someone else’s head, she could *fly!* Soaring high above, swooping down, feeling the wind rush through her feathers, it was a feeling of such utter freedom that has yet to be matched by anything in her life besides the moment she at last achieved a true corporeal form.

One night, drunk on her newfound freedom, Liselle snuck from the ancient fortress her family called home, and, in the cover of darkness, made her way into the Shudderwood. She assured herself it would be a simple flight… But in her heart, she knew she had sought the thrill of the hunt…

Eventually Liselle encountered a stranger, [[Eurwen Olliffe|Eurwen]], surrounded by the corpses of several [Akatas](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/bestiary/monster-listings/aberrations/akata/). The two made brief conversation, but were quickly attacked by the rest of the slain Akatas’ pack mates. In the commotion, however, a strange, mole-faced spellcaster fled from where he had been observing them. When Eurwen gave chase, Liselle, not wanting to abandon one of the first people outside of her family to have actually met the real her to the depravity and danger of the Shudderwood, Liselle quickly followed.

In the course of the battle against this renegade mage, however, Liselle’s familiar was struck down. Left with no recourse but to beg Eurwen’s aid, Liselle spent the next day and a half possessing a simple stone as Eurwen traveled from the Shudderwood back to Keep Lupin-Baudelaire. It was one of the kindest things anyone had ever done for Liselle, and even then, she was utterly smitten by this strange new acquaintance. Liselle’s family, however, were *far* less understanding… It was all she could do to keep them from coming to blows.

At last, she invited Eurwen to join the family for dinner, so that she could properly express her gratitude and explain to her mother what had happened to her.

## Dinner and Consequences

Their meal was awkward. Mother was, unfortunately, Mother, and would not stop being herself anytime soon. Though she tried her best to make small talk, she felt a distinct and previously unknown discomfort, smiling and conversing with Eurwen through Bernadette’s form. As the meal came to a close, Liselle was instructed to see her guest safely from the keep and to then return immediately to her mother’s study. Not one to disobey such a direct command from her mother, she did.
