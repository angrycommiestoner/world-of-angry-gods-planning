Bokken is a mad hermit who lives in a hut on the edge of the Greenbelt. His half-mad experiments with the alchemical reagents of the region often result in surprisingly potent potions

Having first encountered Bokken in the courtyard of [[Oleg|Oleg’s]] outpost, he appeared suddenly in the predawn hours on the morning of [[Happs Bydon|Happs Bydon’s]] attack, and was just as quickly chased away by Oleg, fearing for the old man’s wellbeing.

After defending Oleg’s trading post, however, the party seeks out the odd hermit who lives to the west, and find him tending to a thriving garden outside a small, ramshackle hut. After some convincing, Bokken invites the adventurers inside, requesting that they seek out Fangberries for him to use in his alchemy.

When they return with not just the berries he asked for, but cuttings from which he could grow his own plants, the old hermit was overjoyed. Expressing his gratitude, he promised to provide them with any less potent elixirs he happens to have laying about free of charge any time they stop by (1d6 potions of cure minor wounds per week between visits, up to a total cap of the party’s level, 1d4+1 healing, cures bleed, confused, dazzled, deafened, fatigued, or sickened conditions) and to sell any of his other potions to them at half price (Potions from the Druid spell list up to level 3)!
